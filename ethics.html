Ethical Use of AI

Guidelines for responsible AI usage in academe, research, and daily contexts.

Responsible Use in the Academe

Responsible Use in the Academe: Involves upholding academic integrity, using technology like AI ethically as a tool for enhancement rather than replacement, and respecting others' privacy and intellectual work.

Avoiding Plagiarism and Misuse

Avoiding Plagiarism and Misuse: Plagiarism happens when you copy someone’s work without giving credit.

Accuracy and Verification of AI Outputs

Accuracy and Verification: AI can make mistakes. Always verify every important detail with trusted sources.

Bias and Fairness

Bias and Fairness: AI systems may inherit biases from training data, leading to unfair outcomes.

Recommended Guidelines

Guidelines:By following these principles, the academic community can harness AI's benefits responsibly while preserving originality, fairness, and intellectual rigor.

Academe Checklist
  • Educators and students should understand and critically evaluate generative AI tools
  • Uses before incorporating them into coursework or research.
  • Recognize when AI is used and assess the reliability of its outputs
  • Be aware of the social and ethical implications of these technologies
  • Communicate transparently about AI assistance in academic work
Plagiarism Checklist
  • Plagiarism includes direct copying, poor paraphrasing, self-plagiarism, and improper citation
  • AI-generated content must be disclosed and properly cited
  • AI plagiarism checkers detect similarities and improve originality
  • Run plagiarism checks at multiple drafting stages
Accuracy Checklist
  • Check numbers, dates, names, and definitions
  • Evaluate relevance, factual accuracy, clarity, and completeness
  • Cross-check AI responses with trusted sources
  • Use fact-checkers and verification tools
Bias Checklist
  • Bias can be based on race, ethnicity, gender, or other attributes
  • Diversify datasets when developing tools
  • Use fairness audits and bias detection techniques
  • Maintain transparency in AI decision processes
  • Ensure fair treatment across demographic groups
Guidelines Checklist
  • Always disclose AI assistance in academic work
  • Substantially rework AI-generated content
  • Properly cite AI tools and sources
  • Verify accuracy and relevance of AI outputs
  • Be aware of AI bias
  • Use AI to enhance learning, not replace it

Note for teachers and parents

Set clear expectations for AI use, invite questions about mistakes or bias in AI outputs, and model how to use these tools as study aids instead of shortcuts.
