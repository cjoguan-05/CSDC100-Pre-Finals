What is Generative AI?

Generative AI learns from large datasets and can create new text, images, code, audio, or video based on your prompt.

Where is Generative AI used?

Common ways students and professionals use GenAI today.

School and learning

Explaining concepts, summarizing readings, generating practice questions, and planning study schedules.

Work and productivity

Drafting emails, creating reports, preparing presentations, and automating routine writing tasks.

Creative projects

Brainstorming stories, designing visuals, writing lyrics, and exploring new ideas for art or media.

Daily tasks

Planning trips, organizing to do lists, rewriting text, or summarizing long documents.

How does Generative AI work?

Generative AI models are trained on large collections of text, code, or images. They learn patterns in that data and then predict what comes next when you type a prompt.

The model does not think or understand like a person. It provides likely responses based on patterns it has seen before, so its answers always need checking.

1

Data and patterns

The model trains on large datasets and learns how words and ideas appear together.

2

Model training

Many layers of artificial neurons adjust to predict the next token in a sequence.

3

Prompt and response

You give a prompt, the model predicts likely outputs, and returns a response you can review and edit.

Before you use AI
  • Check your school or course policy on AI tools.
  • Do not submit AI generated content as your own work.
  • Verify important facts with trusted sources.
  • Avoid sharing passwords, IDs, or sensitive personal data.

AI can support your learning, but you are still responsible for the quality and honesty of your work.
